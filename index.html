Executive Thesis

Artificial intelligence has entered a new infrastructure constraint cycle.

For the past decade, performance scaled with compute. That model is breaking. The limiting factor is no longer compute alone. It is movement.

As AI systems scale, the cost of moving data exceeds the cost of processing it. Interconnect bandwidth, switching speed, and energy efficiency are now the dominant constraints.

Electronic systems are reaching physical limits:

  • heat generation
  • resistance
  • signal latency
  • power density
This is driving a structural shift toward:
OPTICAL AND PHOTONIC INFRASTRUCTURE

The Infrastructure Shift

Optical systems use light instead of electrons to move and process data.

This enables significantly higher bandwidth, lower latency, and reduced energy consumption. Photonic processors and optical switching are moving from research into deployment.

This is not theoretical. It is already being integrated into AI and high-performance computing systems.

The New Stack

Next-generation compute is being redefined across three layers:

Processing Layer: Optical Processing Units (OPUs) and heterogeneous XPUs

Interconnect Layer: Optical circuit switching (OCS), photonic buses, and fabric architectures

System Layer: Clusters, arrays, and distributed compute systems built around optical movement

Performance is no longer defined by compute. It is defined by how efficiently data moves through the system.

Category Formation

This defines a new category: movement-constrained compute infrastructure.

The naming layer for this category is forming now. Naming decisions determine architecture identity, platform positioning, and long-term control of system language. Once infrastructure is deployed and standardized, naming becomes fixed.
